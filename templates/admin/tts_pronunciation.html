How the TTS text pipeline works
Before any text is sent to the speech engine it passes through four layers of normalization in order:
  1. Time expansion — compact clock times are converted to spoken words (1100 PM → eleven o'clock PM, 9:30 AM → nine thirty AM).
  2. NWS-specific cleanup — three built-in rules handle patterns unique to NOAA/NWS alert text:
    • Alternate-timezone slash notation is stripped (/5 PM CDT/ → 5 PM CDT).
    • ST. is expanded to Saint (e.g. Saint Joseph).
    • Indiana county-name disambiguation: ALLEN IN → ALLEN Indiana, guarded by a county name look-up so IN EFFECT and section headers like IN MICHIGAN are never touched.
  3. Built-in acronym table — hard-coded expansions for EAS/NWS tokens, timezone abbreviations, and US state codes used as county-name markers (MI → Michigan, OH → Ohio, AFD → Air Force Depot, …).
  4. This dictionary — your custom rules below are applied last, longest-first, so multi-word entries are never masked by shorter ones.
Use the Pronunciation Preview panel on the TTS Settings page to test any text through the full pipeline before going live.
