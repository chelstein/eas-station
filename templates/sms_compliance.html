SMS Messaging Policy

Last updated: {{ current_time().strftime('%B %d, %Y') }}

EAS Station sends SMS messages exclusively for emergency alert notifications. Messages are sent only to phone numbers explicitly configured by the system administrator in the EAS Station admin panel. This is not a public subscription service.

1. Program Description

EAS Station is a self-hosted Emergency Alert System (EAS) research and monitoring platform operated by a licensed amateur radio operator. When SMS notifications are enabled by the system administrator, EAS Station sends text message alerts to pre-configured recipients whenever an EAS alert is received and broadcast by the system. Messages contain the emergency event code, alert headline, affected area identifiers, and timestamp.

SMS messaging is powered by Twilio using a toll-free or long-code phone number provisioned by the system operator.

Use case: Emergency public-safety alert notifications only. No marketing, promotional, or non-emergency messages are sent through this program.

2. Opt-In / Consent Disclosure

Because EAS Station is self-hosted software operated by a system administrator (not a public-facing subscription service), opt-in is managed at the operator level:

  • The system administrator adds recipient phone numbers directly to the EAS Station admin panel under Settings › Notifications.
  • Only individuals who have provided explicit prior written consent to the system operator may be added as SMS recipients.
  • Adding a phone number constitutes the operator's attestation that the individual has consented to receive EAS alert SMS messages from this program.
  • Consent must be obtained and documented before any messages are sent.

Consent Disclosure Language (shown to recipients at opt-in):

By providing your phone number you agree to receive EAS emergency alert SMS notifications from EAS Station. These alerts are issued by the National Weather Service and authorized government agencies. Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency varies. Reply STOP to stop messages at any time. Reply HELP for help. See the Privacy Policy and Terms of Use.

3. Message Frequency

Message frequency depends entirely on the volume of EAS alerts received by the system. EAS alerts are issued by the National Weather Service and other authorized government agencies. During severe weather events or other emergencies, multiple messages may be sent within a short period. During quiet periods, no messages may be sent at all.

Message frequency varies. You may receive multiple messages per day during active alert periods.

4. Message & Data Rates

Message and data rates may apply.

Standard SMS and data rates charged by your mobile carrier may apply to messages received from this system. EAS Station and its operators do not charge any fee for SMS notifications.

5. Sample Message Format

Messages follow this format (actual content varies per alert):

EAS ALERT: TOR
Tornado Warning for Putnam County
Areas: 039137
2026-03-12T14:00:00
- EAS Station
Reply STOP to stop msgs

All messages include an opt-out reminder (Reply STOP to stop msgs) as required by CTIA messaging guidelines.

6. How to Opt Out (STOP)

To stop receiving SMS messages from this system at any time, reply STOP to any message. You will receive a one-time confirmation and will no longer receive SMS notifications from this number.

You may also contact the system operator directly to have your number removed from the recipient list.

Keyword Effect
STOPUnsubscribe from all messages
STOP ALLUnsubscribe and block all future messages
CANCELUnsubscribe from all messages
ENDUnsubscribe from all messages
QUITUnsubscribe from all messages
UNSUBSCRIBEUnsubscribe from all messages

These keywords are processed automatically at the carrier level by Twilio. No additional action is needed from the recipient.

7. Help

Reply HELP to any message for assistance. Twilio will return basic service information. You may also contact the system operator directly through the contact information they have provided.

HELP — Receive help information and operator contact details

8. Supported Carriers

SMS messages are delivered via Twilio and are compatible with all major U.S. wireless carriers, including but not limited to:

  • AT&T
  • T-Mobile
  • Verizon Wireless
  • US Cellular
  • Boost Mobile
  • Cricket Wireless
  • And other regional and national carriers

Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages.

9. Privacy

Phone numbers added to the EAS Station SMS recipient list are stored in the local EAS Station database on the operator's infrastructure. Numbers are shared with Twilio, Inc. solely for the purpose of delivering SMS messages. Twilio's privacy policy applies to their processing of this data.

The EAS Station project maintainers do not have access to phone numbers stored in any operator's deployment. See the full Privacy Policy for complete data handling details.
Twilio's privacy policy: twilio.com/en-us/legal/privacy.

10. Contact

Questions about SMS messaging or this policy should be directed to the system operator who deployed this EAS Station instance. For questions about the EAS Station software itself, visit the GitHub repository.
