SMS Messaging Policy

Last updated: {{ current_time().strftime('%B %d, %Y') }}

EAS Station sends SMS messages exclusively for emergency alert notifications. Messages are sent only to phone numbers explicitly configured by the system administrator in the EAS Station admin panel. This service is not a public subscription service.

1. Program Description

EAS Station is a self-hosted Emergency Alert System (EAS) research and monitoring platform. When SMS notifications are enabled by the system administrator, EAS Station will send text message alerts to pre-configured recipients whenever an EAS alert is received and broadcast by the system. These messages contain emergency alert type, event code, affected area, and expiration information.

SMS messaging is powered by Twilio using a toll-free or long-code phone number provisioned by the system operator.

2. How Opt-In Works

Because EAS Station is self-hosted software operated by a system administrator (not a public-facing subscription service), opt-in is managed at the operator level:

  • The system administrator adds recipient phone numbers directly to the EAS Station admin panel under Settings › Notifications.
  • Only individuals who have provided explicit, prior written consent to the system operator may be added as SMS recipients.
  • Adding a phone number to the recipient list constitutes the operator's attestation that the individual at that number has consented to receive EAS alert SMS messages from this system.
  • Consent must be obtained before any messages are sent and must be documented by the operator.

3. Message Frequency

Message frequency depends entirely on the volume of EAS alerts received by the system. EAS alerts are issued by the National Weather Service and other authorized government agencies. During severe weather events or other emergencies, multiple messages may be sent within a short period. During quiet periods, no messages may be sent at all.

Message frequency varies. You may receive multiple messages per day during active alert periods.

4. Message & Data Rates

Message and data rates may apply. Standard SMS and data rates charged by your mobile carrier may apply to messages received from this system. EAS Station and its operators do not charge any fee for SMS notifications, but your carrier's standard rates apply.

5. How to Opt Out (STOP)

To stop receiving SMS messages from this system at any time, reply STOP to any message you receive. After texting STOP, you will receive a one-time confirmation message and will no longer receive SMS notifications from this number.

You may also contact the system operator directly to have your number removed from the recipient list in the EAS Station admin panel.

STOP — Unsubscribe from all messages
STOP ALL — Unsubscribe and block all future messages

6. Help

For assistance, reply HELP to any message you receive from this system. You may also contact the system operator directly through the contact information they have provided.

HELP — Receive help information and operator contact details

7. Supported Carriers

SMS messages are delivered via Twilio and are compatible with all major U.S. wireless carriers, including but not limited to:

  • AT&T
  • T-Mobile
  • Verizon Wireless
  • Sprint (now part of T-Mobile)
  • US Cellular
  • Boost Mobile
  • Cricket Wireless
  • And other regional and national carriers

Carriers are not liable for delayed or undelivered messages.

8. Privacy

Phone numbers added to the EAS Station SMS recipient list are stored in the local EAS Station database on the operator's infrastructure. Numbers are shared with Twilio solely for the purpose of delivering SMS messages. Twilio's privacy policy applies to their processing of this data.

The EAS Station maintainers (project authors) do not have access to phone numbers stored in any operator's deployment. See our full Privacy Policy for more information.

9. Contact

Questions about SMS messaging or this policy should be directed to the system operator who deployed this EAS Station instance. For questions about the EAS Station software itself, visit the project on GitHub.

For Twilio-related questions, visit Twilio's Privacy Policy.
