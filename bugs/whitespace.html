Select up to 31 SAME location codes to monitor. The nationwide code (000000) is automatically included and required for nationwide alerts.

This system serves two distinct user groups with different needs:

EAS Broadcasting System

For: Broadcasters, radio/TV stations, cable operators

Purpose: Forward Emergency Alert System (EAS) messages to the public

Coverage: Your county + adjoining counties (regional coverage area)

Action: Triggers audio EAS broadcasts when alerts match configured zones

Geographic Alert Storage System

For: Emergency managers, EMA directors, public safety officials, first responders

Purpose: Store alerts, calculate geographic boundaries, display on maps

Coverage: Your local county/jurisdiction only

Action: Saves to database, calculates polygon intersections, shows on emergency management dashboard

Why the distinction matters:

Broadcasters need to relay alerts for multiple counties (their coverage area), but emergency managers only need detailed geographic data for their specific jurisdiction.

Processing and storing geographic boundaries for dozens of adjoining counties would waste resources and clutter the emergency management interface.

Configuration Fields Explained:
  • SAME / FIPS Counties: Your local county codes (e.g., 039137 for Putnam County, OH). Always triggers BOTH broadcast AND storage.
  • NOAA Zone / UGC Codes (All Monitored): All zones to monitor including your county and adjoining areas (e.g., OHZ003 for Putnam + OHZ009 for Hancock). Used for EAS broadcasting.
  • Storage Zone Codes (Local Only): ONLY your local county zones (e.g., OHZ003, OHC137 for Putnam). Determines which UGC-based alerts get stored and have boundaries calculated.

Snow Emergency Management

Manage snow emergency levels for Putnam County and adjacent counties in northwest Ohio.

Ohio Snow Emergency Levels Explained

Ohio sheriffs use a standardized three-level snow emergency system to communicate road conditions and travel advisories:

No Emergency

Normal driving conditions. No restrictions on travel. Roads may still be slippery in winter weather.

Level 1

Roadways are hazardous with blowing and drifting snow. Roads may also be icy. Motorists are urged to drive very cautiously.

Level 2

Roadways are hazardous. Only those who feel it is necessary to drive should be out. Contact your employer to see if you should report to work.

Level 3

All roadways are closed to non-emergency personnel. Those traveling on the roadways may subject themselves to arrest.

Note: During a Level 3 emergency, only emergency personnel and essential workers should be on the roads. Violators may be ticketed or arrested, and tow trucks may not respond to non-emergency calls.

Configuration: VFD display settings are managed in Environment Variables under the "VFD Display" category. Configure serial port (VFD_PORT) and baud rate (VFD_BAUDRATE).

OLED Display (Argon Industria SSD1306)

I2C OLED Display Module integrated with the Argon Industria case. Provides compact, high-contrast status and alert information on a 128×64 pixel display.

Configuration: OLED display settings are managed in Environment Variables under the "OLED Display" category. Enable/disable, I2C bus address (OLED_I2C_ADDRESS), resolution, and rotation settings.
GPIO Pins Reserved: Physical header pins 1-8 (BCM pins 2, 3, 4, 14 plus power/ground) are reserved for the Argon OLED enclosure. These pins cannot be used for other GPIO functions.

Zigbee Smart Home (Argon Industria V5 Module)

Zigbee Coordinator Module for controlling smart lights, sirens, switches, and sensors during emergency alerts. The Argon Industria V5 Zigbee module connects via UART to your Raspberry Pi.

Configuration: Zigbee module settings are managed in Environment Variables under the "Zigbee Module" category. Configure serial port (ZIGBEE_PORT), baud rate, channel, and PAN ID.
Monitoring Available: View coordinator status, connected devices, and diagnostics on the Zigbee Monitoring page. Configure the module in Environment Variables, then monitor real-time status and device activity.
Configure Zigbee Module
Serial port, baud rate, channel, and network settings
View Zigbee Monitoring
Real-time coordinator status, devices, and diagnostics
Recommended Configuration:
  • ZIGBEE_PORT=/dev/ttyAMA0 - Default UART port for Argon module
  • ZIGBEE_BAUDRATE=115200 - Standard speed for Zigbee coordinators
  • ZIGBEE_CHANNEL=15 - Avoids WiFi interference (channels 1, 6, 11)
  • ZIGBEE_PAN_ID - Leave empty for auto-generated network ID
